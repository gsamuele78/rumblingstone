## SETTING: CANNATH VALE (Forgotten Realms — Elsir Vale Adaptation)

The RumblingStone campaign uses *Red Hand of Doom* (Jacobs & Wyatt, 2006) as its adventure
path, adapted wholesale into the **Forgotten Realms, 1372 DR**. The original Elsir Vale has
been remapped to **Cannath Vale** in the Eastern Dalelands. For the complete location mapping,
NPC adaptation, faction notes, and regional encounter tables, see:

→ `skills/forgotten-realms-lore/references/fr-cannath-vale.md`

## CORE RULE MODIFICATIONS

### Combat
- **Massive Damage Rule**: ACTIVE. 50+ HP damage in one hit → Fort DC 15 or die (PHB p. 145)
- **5-foot step**: Can be taken even on a full attack (RAW confirmed)
- **Flanking**: Requires an unobstructed line between both flankers passing through the target
- **Death at negative HP**: Standard −10 threshold (not CON-based variant)
- **Stable recovery**: DC 10 Heal check (standard action) to stabilize; 10% natural/round

### Skills
- **Diplomacy**: Using the reaction track (Hostile → Unfriendly → Neutral → Friendly → Helpful).
  Each category shift requires a separate successful check.
- **Forgery**: DC 15 for simple documents, DC 20 for official seals, DC 25 for arcane documents

### Magic
- **Sorcerer metamagic**: Full-round action (RAW) to apply metamagic spontaneously
- **Concentration while moving**: Not allowed during standard move action
- **Shadow Weave**: Available for Shar-worshippers; see fr-feats.md for mechanics

### Death and Healing
- **Negative HP**: Using −10 standard threshold
- **Hero's Last Stand (House Rule)**: Once per character per campaign, at the moment of death
  (reaching −10 HP), a character may make a DC 20 Concentration check to act one final round
  before falling. On success, the character completes one action, then falls dying. This represents
  the "last heroic act" trope.

## EXPERIENCE POINTS

**Track**: Standard (not Slow or Fast)
**Method**: Challenge-based; award per encounter at session end
**Milestone Bonuses** (per RHoD chapter climax):
- Chapter 1 climax (Skull Gorge): +500 XP per PC
- Chapter 2 climax (Lhesper): +800 XP per PC
- Chapter 3 climax (Ghostlord Lair → Vraath Keep): +600 XP per PC
- Chapter 4 climax (Battle of Rethmar): +1,200 XP per PC
- Final (Fane of Tiamat): +2,000 XP per PC

## LOOT AND WEALTH

- **Division**: Fair share; magic items appraised by party caster first
- **Contested items**: Roll-off (d20 + relevant ability mod); winner gets +1 priority on next
  contested roll, so no one always wins
- **Crafting**: Allowed between sessions at standard time/cost; must have downtime narrative
- **NPC shops in Rethmar**: planned file at `campaign/lore/rethmar-shops.md` (not yet created — TBD)

## ALIGNMENT NOTES

- **Paladin fall**: Knowingly performing an evil act + refusing Atonement → fall. DM will signal
  grey areas before imposing consequences.
- **Detect Evil**: Works on creatures with active evil intent, not on alignment aura alone.
  A neutral merchant thinking selfish thoughts does not ping.
- **Evil PCs**: Not permitted without group agreement. Morally grey is fine; actively undermining
  the party is not.

## SESSION LOGISTICS

- **Recap**: First 5 minutes of each session, a player (rotating) gives the in-character recap
- **Notes**: The campaign wiki lives in `campaign/sessions/`; each session gets a markdown file
- **Absent players**: PCs present but "hanging back"; cannot take combat actions; receive 50% XP

## CANNATH VALE REGIONAL RULES

### The Wyrmbones Weather (Flamerule — Campaign Start)
- 30% chance of afternoon thunderstorm each day; on a 1 on the d20 during outdoor travel → encounter
- Fog over the Lhespenbog every dawn until 1 hour post-sunrise; Spot/Listen DCs increase by +4
- Extended overcast from Eleasis 1 onwards (the horde's fire magic changes local weather)

### Lhespenbog (Lhespenbog equivalent)
- Difficult terrain throughout; movement costs 2 squares per 1 square in shallow areas
- Standing water: Disease check (Fort DC 12) after each full day of wading
- Black dragon Regiarix's acid residue: +1d4 acid damage to unprotected metal per exposure

### Shaarcah Forest (Shaarcah Forest equivalent)
- Wild elves hostile until the party proves hostile to the horde (Diplomacy DC 20 to open dialogue)
- Pass Without Trace is ambient here; tracking DCs increase by +5 inside the forest boundary
- Teleportation and scrying: 20% failure chance inside the Shaarcah Forest (elven ward-magic)
