# Forgotten Realms — Artifacts & Unique Magic Items (D&D 3.5)

Sources: FRCS (2001), Faiths & Pantheons (2002), various FR sourcebooks

Note: Artifacts cannot be created by mortal means and cannot be destroyed by normal means.
Each has a listed method of destruction. Minor artifacts are less powerful than major ones.
All values listed are lore-accurate from 3.5 publications; no copyrighted text is reproduced verbatim.

## MAJOR ARTIFACTS OF FAERÛN

### The Nether Scrolls
**Type**: Set of 100 major artifacts
**Origin**: Created by the Sarrukh (Creator Race); discovered by the Netherese in −2954 DR
**Appearance**: Scrolls of silvery metal, covered in sarrukh runes; unrollable by normal means
**Powers**: Each scroll contains Sarrukh magical knowledge. The set contains the secrets of all
magic — spell construction, wild magic, the Weave itself. They are why Netheril rose to power.
**Location (1372 DR)**: Scattered; portions held by the Shadovar of Shade Enclave; a few are
in the Anauroch ruins; several are completely lost
**Destruction**: Cannot be destroyed; they have survived the Fall of Netheril intact

### The Tablets of Fate
**Type**: Major artifact pair
**Origin**: Created by Ao at the dawn of time
**Appearance**: Two large stone tablets inscribed with divine law
**Powers**: Define the responsibilities and limits of divine power in Ao's cosmology.
While Ao held them, no deity could exceed their defined portfolio. Their theft by Bane and Myrkul
caused the Time of Troubles.
**Location (1372 DR)**: Secured by Ao; not accessible to mortals or gods
**Significance**: The most cosmologically important objects in Faerûn

### The Hand and Eye of Vecna
**Type**: Major artifacts (technically from Greyhawk but present in multiversal lore)
**Powers**: The Hand: +2 STR; claws deal 1d10 + 2d6 cold damage; can cast various necromantic spells.
The Eye: Replaces wearer's eye; see in all conditions; gaze attacks; True Seeing at will
**Price**: Must cut off your own hand/gouge out your own eye to attune them
**Note**: These are crossover artifacts. A DM may or may not allow them in FR campaigns.

### The Crenshinibon (Crystal Shard)
**Type**: Major artifact
**Origin**: Created by evil wizards and priests using negative energy; possibly demonic influence
**Appearance**: A shard of glittering crystal about 1 foot long
**Powers**: Amplifies power of any wielder. Can create crystal towers (Cryshal-Tirith) that serve
as fortresses. Draws power from sunlight. Corrupts the wielder toward evil ambitions.
Grants enhanced magical powers, tactical knowledge, and megalomaniacal tendencies.
**Location (1372 DR)**: Destroyed in the Drizzt novels (thrown into the sun-gate of Tarterus);
may still exist in campaigns where those events haven't occurred
**Destruction**: Destruction in direct sunlight (hence the plot of the relevant novel)

## ARTIFACTS ASSOCIATED WITH FR DEITIES

### The Wand of Orcus
**Origin**: Orcus (demon prince of undeath)
**Powers**: Any living creature struck by the wand must make Fort DC 30 or die. Can raise
unlimited undead. Orcus lost it briefly (when Kiaransalee slew him). It was recovered.
**Location**: The Abyss; occasionally appears on Faerûn

### Moander's Vessel
**Origin**: Moander (dead god of corruption)
**Powers**: Corrupts and animates plant matter; creates twisted plant-zombie creatures.
Moander's essence is imprisoned in a monolith form.
**Location**: Varies per novel continuity; treat as destroyed/imprisoned

### Selûne's Tear
**Type**: Minor artifact
**Origin**: Wept by Selûne during her first battle with Shar
**Appearance**: A clear teardrop-shaped gem that glows faintly
**Powers**: Grants the wielder protection against undead; +4 sacred bonus to Turn Undead;
Selûne's Blessing (Protection from Evil 3/day); can locate lycanthropes within 1 mile

### The Darkbolt of Shar
**Type**: Minor artifact (several may exist)
**Appearance**: A bolt of dark metal with a purple sheen
**Powers**: When fired from any crossbow, deals 3d6 shadow damage + 3d6 against good
creatures; on a critical hit, the target must save vs. permanent blindness

## LEGENDARY WEAPONS OF FAERÛN

### The Moonblades
**Type**: Unique elven swords (many exist; each is unique)
**Origin**: Created by elven artisans of the First Flowering
**Appearance**: Slender elven longswords, typically silver-white, that glow faintly in moonlight
**Bonding**: Each Moonblade bonds to a specific elven bloodline. Attempts by non-bonded individuals
to use the blade result in death (Fort DC 25 or die). The blade absorbs the power of each wielder
who dies bonded to it, becoming more powerful over generations.
**Powers (example — Arilyn Moonblade)**:
- +5 enhancement
- Defender quality (transfer enhancement to AC)
- Elfshadow (create a duplicate of the wielder as a companion)
- Various powers accumulated from previous wielders (DM determines)
**Location**: Scattered among elven noble families and lost ruins

### Blackstaff (Azoun's Staff)
**Note**: The Blackstaff is Khelben Arunsun's personal staff. It is a unique magic staff, not
technically an artifact, but legendary in Waterdeep.
**Powers**: +3 staff; absorb magic (counterspell); various spell effects at higher CL; contains
Khelben's imprisoned soul (partially) as a contingency

### The Sword of Mystra
**Type**: Minor artifact
**Powers**: Holy avenger equivalent +5; deals 3d6 vs. arcane spellcasters who use it to harm
innocent beings; Detect Magic, Identify at will for wielder; the Weave speaks to wielder
**Note**: Multiple swords of this type exist; given by Mystra to worthy champions

### The Dragon's Eye (unique dagger)
**Powers**: +3 dagger; see invisibility (continuous); detect poison at will; held by a thief
lord in various FR novels; now lost/scattered

## LEGENDARY ARMOR AND SHIELDS

### The Armor of the Fallen Star
**Type**: Full plate, unique
**Powers**: +5 full plate; +4 resistance to fire; Blinding Speed (3/day, as the spell Haste);
made from a fallen star iron; sheds faint silver light in darkness
**Location**: Legendary; associated with a paladin of Torm in Cormyr's history; possibly entombed

### Buckler of Shar's Shadow
**Type**: Minor artifact buckler
**Powers**: +3 buckler; Darkness 3/day; the wielder's shadow attacks opponents (1d6 cold on hit)

## SIGNIFICANT FR MAGIC ITEMS (Non-Artifact)

These are not artifacts but are famous and unique in Faerûn lore.

### Harper Pins
Simple silver crescent-and-harp pins worn by Harpers. Functions:
- Identify the wearer as a Harper to other Harpers (Sense Motive DC 15 to identify without training)
- Act as a mild curse to anyone who steals one and wears it without being a Harper (Feeblemind 1/week check)
- Some rare versions are also Minor Sending devices (can send messages as per Sending to another
specific pin, once per day)

### Red Wizard Tattoos (Thayan)
Magical tattoos created through the Scribe Tattoo feat. Not artifacts, but notable:
- Each tattoo occupies the "skin" magic item slot
- A Red Wizard may have up to 5 tattoos simultaneously (DM may vary)
- Typical effects: +1 CL for specialist school, resistance to one energy type, ability score
enhancements, minor spell-like abilities
- Removing a tattoo deals 3d6 damage; restoring a removed tattoo costs the full creation price

### Zhentarim Talisman
Small iron disk with Bane's symbol (black hand). Functions:
- Identify the wearer as Zhentarim to Network members
- Grant the wearer +2 Intimidate when dealing with people who fear the Zhentarim
- Some versions have a One-Way Gate to a Zhentarim safehouse (DM discretion)

### The Spellbook of Alustriel
Not a single item — Alustriel's spellbooks (multiple volumes) contain every spell she's ever
developed. They are beyond priceless. Most are kept in Silverymoon's Vault of Sages.
Value: 500,000+ gp per volume (if someone could even acquire one)

### The Gauntlets of Ironadan
Legendary dwarven smith's gauntlets:
**Powers**: +4 Craft checks; treat as STR 24 for Craft purposes; 1/day forge any nonmagical item
from raw materials in 1 hour rather than days; can identify metal quality and composition by touch
Location: Citadel Adbar; inherited in the Ironadan dwarf-clan

## CREATING FR-APPROPRIATE MAGIC ITEMS

When creating custom items for the RumblingStone campaign, consider:

**Cultural markers**:
- Cormyrean items: Often marked with purple dragons; often paladin/martial focus
- Dalelands items: Practical, no-nonsense; often forester/ranger themed
- Zhentarim items: Black iron; intimidating; often cursed or corrupted
- Elven (Cormanthor) items: Moonwood or mithral; singing/humming quality; nature-attuned
- Red Hand items (campaign-specific): Hobgoblin make; crude but functional; red lacquer

**Red Hand of Doom specific items** (adapt from RHoD sourcebook, not reproduce):
- Wyrmlord equipment: Leaders carry magical arms; specific items should come from
  your physical RHoD book. Document them in the existing
  `../../rumblingstone-campaign/references/campaign-story-arcs.md`
  (adaptation log) — there is no separate `rhod-adaptations.md`.
- Tiamat cult items: Black/red coloration; fire or shadow attuned

**Pricing reference**: See `../../dnd-35-srd/references/items.md` for standard pricing formulas.
